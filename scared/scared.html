Anxious

It isn't any worse than what
I discover in the dentist's chair
under the nitrous oxide.

The whole jaw is going, I complain, the gums, the bone,
two enormous fillings lost. What do I need?
Maybe a guillotine? says my dentist, the joker.

The only thing I have to fear is fear itself, I tell him.
You believe in that bullshit? he says,
setting to work on my rotting bicuspid.

Now comes the good part. Breathing the happy gas,
I get answers to all the questions I had
about death but was afraid to ask.

Will ther be pain? Yes.
Will my desires be unsatisfied? Yes.
My human potential remain unrealized? Yes.

Can a person stop minding about that? Certainly.
Can I commend my spirit to the seventeen
angels whistling outside the dentist's window?

Of course. How nice the happy gas.
What a good friend.
I unclench my sweaty little hand.

I wave goodbye to my teeth.
It seems they are leaving by train for a vacation.
I'll meet them in the country when I can.

Anxiety About Dying
By Alicia Ostriker

Insecure

He's still young—; thirty, but looks younger—
or does he? . . . In the eyes and cheeks, tonight,
turning in the mirror, he saw his mother,—
puffy; angry; bewildered . . . Many nights
now, when he stares there, he gets angry:—
something unfulfilled there, something dead
to what he once thought he surely could be—
Now, just the glamour of habits . . .
Once,instead,
he thought insight would remake him, he'd reach
—what? The thrill, the exhilaration
unravelling disaster, that seemed to teach
necessary knowledge . . . became just jargon.

Sick of being decent, he craves another
crash. What reaches him except disaster?

Helpless

Helpless and fond, the wolf and lamb
Each a recipient, to each germinal
Rivet together while great empires fall
And shoot new empires in each other's name.
For one is Love and one is Bitter Shame
Or Hatred. Each, forever conjugal,
Forever balanced to the other's scale
Impregnates snow and scatters seed in flame.
Of them is God, resolved to unity-
The planets' cavern, the structured flesh of stars,
The final flesh, the last stockade, the bone
Golden and spherical. Only His bars
Decree the depth to which His Self is sown.

Helpless and Fond, The Wolf and Lamb
By Doris Bailey

Confused

All right, I admit it, I'm to blame,
it's on account of me
the Messiah doesn't come;

I am the blip on the screen,
the cold spot, the dark area you see
with indefinite borders, moving sluggishly

crabwise, with a density all its own,
unabsorbed, indissoluble;the clot
in the body politic-that's me,

accountable by myself (though not alone)
for the tarrying football, for our
continuing bad name:

because of my imperfect faith,
my ritual ommissions, my mistakes in form,
my little games of nor-care-I,

because I am stiff-necked, and push
the quarrel with God one step too far,
preferring to do the thing my way

rather than not at all (unable
to play by the rules to save my life,
unwilling to drop the ball)-

becasue I confuse having a part
with holding apart, and star with shield;
because I will always pause

in my studies along the road, to say
"How far is that field,
how fine is that tree;"
because I have made strange fire
again and again, and lived,
and the earth has not swallowed me.

A Confused Heart
By Robyn Sarah
